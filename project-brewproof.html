The problem

Tracking fluids meant juggling apps that each did one thing. Last Call handled alcohol well. Water Llama tracked water but buried logging under badges and gamification. Nothing for caffeine felt right. The data scattered across three places, so there was no single view of how it all added up, and no easy way to notice how intake actually affected how a day felt.

The real want was simple. One fast place to log all three, see them together, and set your own goals, with no app trying to coach you or score you.

What I built

BrewProof is a focused iOS app that unifies the three into one log and one summary. It was scoped with a Simple to Lovable to Complete framework, so the product earned each layer instead of shipping everything at once:

  • Simple: fast logging for water, caffeine, and alcohol, a today view that shows the balance at a glance, and HealthKit as the home for every fluid type.
  • Lovable: trends and patterns worth coming back for, like a caffeine to water ratio, daily balance, and correlations between intake and how you feel (jittery, sluggish, dehydrated).
  • Complete: the wider surface, more beverage categories, an Apple Watch companion, export, and deeper analytics.

It is built on SwiftUI and SwiftData, with HealthKit integration as a core requirement across every phase and a WidgetKit presence on the home screen. The design borrows the parts of Last Call that worked, fast and minimal logging, your own limits, your data in Apple Health, and drops the gamification that got in the way everywhere else.

What it made possible

One log, one summary, and one honest picture of water, caffeine, and alcohol over time. Because everything writes to Apple Health, the data belongs to the person, not the app, and it stays useful alongside everything else they already track.

Why it matters

BrewProof is here as proof that the systems thinking lands. It starts from a real personal problem, scopes it with a product framework, respects the user enough to skip the badges and the lectures, and ships as a finished, well-made iOS app. Not an experiment or a framework. A product you can hold.
