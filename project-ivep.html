An entity that answers a concept with motion and color instead of words.

The problem

Almost every AI interaction ends in a wall of text. You give a model a concept and it gives you sentences back. But plenty of the things worth expressing, a mood, a feeling, the shape of an idea, do not really want to be sentences. The question I wanted to sit with: what happens when language becomes behavior instead of text?

What I built

IVEP is a fluid, ink-like entity, inspired by the alien language in Arrival, that responds to a written input by changing how it moves, not by writing back. You type a concept and you watch the answer.

  • A visual grammar. The entity has a defined behavior language: shape transformations (expanding, contracting, pulsing, splitting, merging), a color vocabulary, and movement patterns, each mapped to the emotional register of the input.
  • A 3D fluid body. Built in Three.js with physics-based, organic deformation, so the motion reads as a living thing reacting rather than a chart updating.
  • A model in the middle. A FastAPI backend interprets the text into that behavior schema, with support for OpenAI, Anthropic, and Gemini so I could compare how different models read the same concept.
  • Graceful offline mode. When the backend is not there, it falls back to client-side interpretation so the experience never just stops.

What it made possible

A non-verbal channel for an AI response, something you watch instead of read. It also became a small testbed for cross-modal translation and for seeing, side by side, how different models interpret an abstract or emotional prompt when the output has to be behavior rather than prose.

Why it matters

This is the kind of interface work I care about: taking a poetic idea and turning it into a system that actually runs. It is a concrete argument that AI interaction does not have to mean a chat box, and that there is room to design responses you feel before you parse.
